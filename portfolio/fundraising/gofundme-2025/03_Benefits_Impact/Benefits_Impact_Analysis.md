### ‚úÖ SSDI (Social Security Disability Insurance) - NO IMPACT
**Your SSDI is 100% SAFE from GoFundMe donations.**

**Why:**
- SSDI is an **insurance program**, not needs-based
- SSDI is based on work history/credits, NOT financial need
- Personal gifts and crowdfunding donations **DO NOT count** as income for SSDI
- You could be a millionaire and still receive SSDI

**Source:** Social Security Administration rules, Cuddigan Law, Nash Disability Law

**Quote from disability law experts:**
> "If you are receiving Social Security Disability Insurance (SSDI) benefits, a crowdsourcing campaign will have no affect on your benefits. There are no financial restrictions around gifts or fundraising... Other sources of income like a GoFundMe campaign, gifts, income on investments, trust payouts, and even winning the lottery don't count."

### ‚ö†Ô∏è CalFresh (Food Stamps/SNAP) - MOSTLY SAFE (with caution)

**California CalFresh has NO ASSET TEST** (as of January 2024), so GoFundMe funds sitting in your account are generally NOT a problem.

**Income Test Details:**

**Your status:** Household of 1
**Gross income limit:** $2,510/month (200% federal poverty level)
**Net income limit:** $1,255/month

**How GoFundMe is Treated:**

1. **Federal USDA/FNS Guidance:**
   - GoFundMe funds are treated as a **resource** (asset), not income
   - Since California has NO asset test for CalFresh, this is generally safe
   - **Exception:** If you receive repeated monthly donations that look like regular income, caseworker might count it

2. **Personal Gift Exception:**
   - One-time fundraiser for emergency (housing, medical) is typically treated as personal gifts
   - Personal gifts are NOT counted as income for CalFresh

3. **"Things That Are NOT Income" (California CalFresh Guide):**
   - Gifts from friends and relatives
   - One-time emergency assistance
   - Contributions for specific expenses (rent, medical)

**SAFEST APPROACH:**
- Frame campaign as **one-time emergency** for housing deposit
- Use funds within the month received for intended purpose (housing, medical, computer)
- Don't let large amounts sit in bank account across multiple months
- Report to caseworker proactively if asked about changes

## üö® IMPORTANT DISTINCTIONS

### SSDI vs SSI - Make Sure You Know Which You Have

**SSDI (Social Security Disability Insurance):**
- Based on work history
- No asset limits, no income limits from gifts
- GoFundMe is 100% safe

**SSI (Supplemental Security Income):**
- Needs-based program
- $2,000 asset limit ($3,000 for couples)
- GoFundMe CAN jeopardize benefits
- Requires special planning (ABLE account, special needs trust)

**YOUR SITUATION:** You mentioned SSDI - if this is correct, you're in the clear. If you're on SSI, we need a different strategy (ABLE account or spend-down plan).

**Action Item:** Confirm which program you're on. Check your benefit letter - it will say "SSDI" or "SSI" or "Title II" (SSDI) vs "Title XVI" (SSI).

## üí° BEST PRACTICES TO PROTECT BENEFITS

### 1. Campaign Framing (Critical Language)

**DO say:**

## üéØ IRS TAX IMPLICATIONS

### Will You Owe Taxes on GoFundMe?

**Short answer: NO** (for emergency personal fundraiser)

**Long answer:**

1. **Personal Gift Exception:**
   - Personal crowdfunding for medical, housing, emergency expenses = personal gifts
   - Personal gifts are NOT taxable income (to recipient)
   - No 1099-K unless you exceed $5,000 (2024 tax year threshold) or $20,000 (2025+ threshold)

2. **Form 1099-K Threshold:**
   - **2024 tax year (filing in 2025):** $5,000+ triggers 1099-K
   - **2025 tax year (filing in 2026):** $20,000+ OR 200+ transactions
   - Your campaign goal is under $5,000 ‚Üí Likely no 1099-K for 2024 tax year

3. **If You Receive 1099-K:**
   - Report on **Form 1040, Schedule 1:**
     - Part I, Line 8z: Gross proceeds from 1099-K ($[amount])
     - Part II, Line 24z: Adjustment to income ($[same amount], negative)
     - **Net effect: ZERO taxable income**
   - Include note: "Personal gifts for emergency housing and medical expenses per IRS guidance on crowdfunding"

4. **What's Taxable vs Not:**
   - ‚úÖ NOT taxable: Personal gifts, emergency fundraisers (medical, housing, education)
   - ‚ùå TAXABLE: Business crowdfunding, Kickstarter rewards, employer contributions

### Record Keeping for IRS

**Keep for 3 years:**
- GoFundMe campaign page showing purpose (housing deposit, medical, computer)
- Donor comments showing personal gift intent
- Receipts showing how funds were spent on stated purpose

## üõ†Ô∏è SPECIFIC SCENARIOS & ANSWERS

### Scenario 1: "What if I raise $3,000 and Jerry only needs $1,326?"

**CalFresh impact:**
- If you spend excess on stated purposes (Cash App $315, computer $600-800, medical expenses) = NO PROBLEM
- If $1,000+ sits in bank for 2+ months = Might be counted as resource (but CA has no asset test through 2025)
- **Best practice:** Spend on stated purposes, save remainder in separate savings account (not checking), document everything

**SSDI impact:** ZERO, regardless of amount

### Scenario 2: "What if donors give me $5,000 and I get a 1099-K?"

**IRS reporting:**
- Report on Schedule 1 as shown above (net zero)
- Attach statement: "Personal gifts for emergency housing, medical expenses, and work equipment per IRS crowdfunding guidance"

**Benefits impact:**
- SSDI: ZERO
- CalFresh: If spent within month on stated purposes, likely zero

### Scenario 3: "What if contract work starts paying more and I go over $1,550/month?"

**SSDI impact:**
- $1,550/month is the SGA (Substantial Gainful Activity) limit for 2024
- If you earn MORE than $1,550/month from work, SSA will review your case
- You have a 9-month "Trial Work Period" where you can earn any amount without losing SSDI
- After trial work period, if you consistently earn over SGA, SSDI may be suspended (not terminated - you can restart if work ends)
- **Your situation:** $250/month is WELL below $1,550, so no concern

**CalFresh impact:**
- If income exceeds $2,510/month gross, you lose CalFresh eligibility
- But more income means you need less CalFresh, so not a crisis

### Scenario 4: "What if I do another GoFundMe in 6 months?"

**CalFresh risk increases:**
- Multiple crowdfunding campaigns start to look like "income" rather than one-time emergency
- First campaign = emergency gift (safe)
- Second campaign 6 months later = caseworker might question
- **Best practice:** Use GoFundMe only for true emergencies, not ongoing support

**SSDI impact:** Still zero (gifts don't count even if repeated)
