### What is SSI?

**SSI (Supplemental Security Income)** is a needs-based program that supplements income for disabled individuals with limited income and resources.

## ðŸ” CONCURRENT BENEFITS EXPLAINED

### Who Receives Both SSDI and SSI?

People can receive **concurrent benefits** (both SSDI and SSI) if:
1. They qualify for SSDI based on work history
2. Their SSDI payment is LOW (typically under $987/month for 2025)
3. They have limited resources (under $2,000 for individual)

**Example:**
- Person receives SSDI: $600/month
- Countable income: $600 - $20 = $580
- SSI eligibility: $967 - $580 = **$387/month SSI supplement**
- **Total monthly income:** $600 SSDI + $387 SSI = $987

### Why Concurrent Benefits Exist:

SSDI is based on your work history and earnings. If you:
- Became disabled young (limited work history)
- Had low earnings when you worked
- Didn't work long enough for higher SSDI

Then SSDI alone might not be enough to live on, so SSI supplements it up to the FBR.

**You may have unused Trial Work Period months!**

**What is TWP?**

- During TWP, you can earn ANY amount without losing SSDI
- After TWP, you can still earn up to $1,550/month (SGA limit) indefinitely
