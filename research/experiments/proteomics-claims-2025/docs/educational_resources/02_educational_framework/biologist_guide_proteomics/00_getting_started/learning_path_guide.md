## üìä Progress Tracking

### Self-Assessment Questions

**After Week 1** (All Paths):
- Can I explain why we study protein changes in Alzheimer's?
- Do I understand what a p-value means?
- Can I open and run a Jupyter notebook?
- Can I load and explore our dataset?

**After Week 2** (All Paths):
- Can I complete a differential expression analysis?
- Do I understand multiple testing correction?
- Can I interpret correlation results?
- Can I explain results to a colleague?

**Advanced Mastery** (Some Experience/Quick Start):
- Can I choose appropriate methods for new questions?
- Do I understand when statistical assumptions are violated?
- Can I troubleshoot analysis problems?
- Can I write publication-quality methods sections?

### When to Move Faster/Slower

**Move Faster If**:
- Concepts seem familiar quickly
- You're comfortable with technical details
- You have prior statistics/programming experience
- You're pressed for time

**Slow Down If**:
- Concepts feel overwhelming
- You're making many errors
- You want deep understanding
- You're learning for long-term use

## üí° Success Tips for All Paths

### Learning Strategies
- **Active practice**: Don't just read, DO the analyses
- **Teach back**: Explain concepts to yourself or others
- **Connect to research**: Think about your own questions
- **Embrace errors**: Debugging teaches deep understanding

### Time Management
- **Consistent daily practice** beats marathon sessions
- **Set realistic goals** based on your path
- **Track progress** using the checklists
- **Celebrate wins** when you complete milestones

### When Stuck
- **Check troubleshooting guides** first
- **Review biological context** to regain motivation
- **Try explaining the problem** out loud
- **Take breaks** when frustrated
