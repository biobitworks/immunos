## Typical Literature Coverage

### Most Studies Analyze LIMITED Subsets:

#### Common Literature Approaches:

1. **"Core UPS" Studies** (8-12 proteins):
   - Usually just: UBB, UBC, PSMA1, PSMB5, PSMD1, a few E3s
   - Example: Many early Alzheimer's proteomics papers

2. **"Proteasome-focused" Studies** (20-30 proteins):
   - Focus on 20S/26S subunits only
   - Miss E1/E2/E3 enzymes and DUBs
   - Common in degradation-focused papers

3. **"Selected markers" Studies** (5-10 proteins):
   - Cherry-pick known proteins: UCHL1, USP14, PARK7
   - Miss systematic coverage
   - Most clinical studies use this approach

### Literature Examples Found:

From our searches, studies typically report:
- **"UBE2/4 family, UBA3B, UBA6, UCHL1, PSMA1/3/5 and PSMC4/5, PSMD4/6/7/13"** - ~15 proteins
- **"PSMA1-7, PSMB1-7"** - 14 proteins (core proteasome only)
- **Individual E3 ligases** - Usually 1-5 proteins per study

### What Literature MISSES:

1. **Comprehensive E3 ligase coverage**
   - Most studies: 2-5 E3 ligases
   - Our analysis: 19 E3 ligases

2. **Full deubiquitinase panel**
   - Most studies: 3-5 DUBs (usually UCHL1, USP14, few others)
   - Our analysis: 28 DUBs

3. **Alternative modifiers**
   - Rarely included: SUMO, NEDD8, UFM1, URM1
   - Our analysis: All included
