# City University of Hong Kong (CityUHK)

## Quick Summary

Public research university in Hong Kong with strong programs in biomedical sciences, including virology, aging biology, and computational biology. Home to Prof. Peter Lidsky's research on pathogen control theory of aging.

## Overview

### Mission Statement
City University of Hong Kong aspires to become a leading global university, excelling in research and professional education. It is committed to nurturing and developing students' talents and creating applicable knowledge to support social and economic advancement.

### History and Background
- **Founded**: 1984 as City Polytechnic of Hong Kong
- **University status**: Granted in 1994
- **Current status**: Comprehensive public research university
- **Ranking**: Consistently ranked among top universities in Asia

### Organizational Structure
- **Colleges**: Engineering, Business, Liberal Arts and Social Sciences, Science, Veterinary Medicine and Life Sciences, Law, Creative Media, Data Science, Energy and Environment
- **Department of interest**: Department of Biomedical Sciences
- **Research centers**: Various interdisciplinary research centers

## Focus Areas and Research

### Primary Research Themes

#### Biomedical Sciences
**Description**: Department of Biomedical Sciences conducts research in virology, aging biology, immunology, cancer biology, and infectious diseases.

**Key Projects**:
- Virus-host interactions
- Aging biology and cellular senescence
- Pathogen control mechanisms
- Infectious disease research

**Notable Researchers**: [[../researchers/lidsky-peter|Prof. Peter V. Lidsky]]

#### Aging Biology and Cellular Senescence
**Description**: Research on fundamental mechanisms of aging, with novel theoretical approaches like the pathogen control theory.

**Unique Perspective**: Home to researchers proposing alternative evolutionary frameworks for understanding aging, particularly the connection between pathogen defense and senescence.

**Connection to our research**: Highly relevant to [[../datasets/cellage|CellAge]] and [[../datasets/genage|GenAge]] database analyses.

#### Educational Technology
**Recent development**: Teaching Development Grants supporting innovative educational platforms like GeneBox for interactive biology education.

### Methodological Strengths
- Virology and infectious disease research
- Evolutionary biology approaches
- Theoretical model development
- Interactive educational platforms
