## Quick Summary

This paper uses spatial transcriptomics to investigate age-related inflammation in mouse brain white matter, providing spatial context for inflammatory processes during brain aging.

### Connection to Existing Work

   - Both use spatial transcriptomics for brain tissue

   - Could compare aging signatures between mouse and human

   - Genes identified here can be mapped to protein interaction networks
   - Identify inflammatory pathway modules in aging brain

3. **[[../../research/datasets/genage|GenAge]]** - Cross-reference aging genes
   - Are GenAge longevity genes expressed spatially in aging brain?
   - White matter inflammation connections to systemic aging

4. **[[../../research/datasets/cellage|CellAge]]** - Senescence markers
   - Do senescence genes show spatial patterns in aging white matter?
   - Connection between cellular senescence and inflammation

- **Q3**: Do CellAge senescence markers correlate with white matter inflammation sites?
- **Q4**: Can we identify novel aging biomarkers from spatial expression patterns?
